Ideology over Science
Palm Beach Post Editorial
Thursday, July 8, 2004

W. David Hager, a Kentucky obstetrician and gynecologist, has refused to prescribe contraceptives for single women and recommends Scripture readings as treatment for premenstrual syndrome, headaches, postpartum depression and eating disorders. He bases his practice on ideology, not science, which explains why the like-minded President Bush reappointed him to a critical Food and Drug Administration advisory panel.

Before he first was appointed two years ago to the Reproductive Health Drugs Advisory Committee, Dr. Hager had petitioned the FDA to block the use of RU-486. His goal of getting the FDA to reverse its approval of the alternative to surgical abortion could hamper development of the drug as a treatment for other conditions such as brain tumors, ovarian and uterine cancer, prostate cancer and fibroid tumors. This year, Dr. Hager was one of four who voted against the FDA's approval of over-the-counter sales of emergency contraceptive pills -- ignoring the advice of the American Academy of Pediatrics, the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists and the American Public Health Association.

His reasoning no doubt can be found in Stress and the Woman's Body, the book Dr. Hager and his wife co-wrote, suggesting that women use Scripture to cure their physical ailments. For headaches, Dr. Hager recommends Matthew 13:44-46 ("The kingdom of heaven is like unto treasure hid in a field"). For premenstrual syndrome, he suggests Romans 5:1-11 ("Tribulation worketh patience"). For postpartum depression, he reminds mothers of Psalm 34:18 ("The Lord is nigh unto them that are of a broken heart; and saveth such as be of a contrite spirit"), Revelation 21:4 ("And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more... pain") or II Samuel 22:29 ("For thou art my lamp, O Lord; and the Lord will lighten my darkness").

As a bipartisan group of representatives wrote to President Bush, "Dr. Hager's blatant opposition to so many safe and legal options makes him unfit to serve on this key advisory committee." Mr. Bush's appointment of Dr. Hager, though, was predictable; he routinely ignores fact in the bogus name of divine leadership. It all brings to mind this biblical admonition: "Beware of false prophets, which come to you in sheep's clothing, but inwardly they are ravening wolves." Beware, especially, of false prophets who come along during election years.
