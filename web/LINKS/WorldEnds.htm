World's Abrupt End Goes Largely Unnoticed

LONDON (AheadOfNews.com) - Christians calling themselves "born again" celebrated today as the world suddenly ended at midnight Tuesday, Greenwich Mean Time.

A tambourine player shook his instrument in Hyde Park, where believers with upturned faces awaited an ascent to Heaven.

"We're all set and ready to go," said Donald Marchbanks, dressed in a Rapture Ready T-shirt. "We always said this would be one of the most astonishing events in history."

Meanwhile, most life continued as usual. British Prime Minister Tony Blair spoke enthusiastically about the reconstruction of Syria after the war. Whale watchers reported seeing an unusually large pod of orcas off the coast of northern Scotland. U.S. President George W. Bush announced plans to eliminate taxes entirely and to "just print money as needed." Scientists noted that the Red Sea was a centimeter lower than expected. Four million protestors marched in Jakarta, Indonesia, carrying small red signs saying, "Not."

Light rain is forecast in London tomorrow. Winds should be light at about 10 kilometers per hour from the southwest.
