"Hidden Masonic Symbols in Diego Rivera's SF Frescoes"

Masha Zakheiim will be our speaker this month and will be speculating on the atheistic elements of Diego Rivera's life, of which she has several examples-more anecdotal than art-illustrated.  Masha Zakheiim, a San Francisco native, is the daughter of Bernard Zakheiim, a local artist of the 1930s. Though her academic degrees are in English Literature, she later expanded her studies to include Humanities when she taught for many years at City College of SF. The college owns an amazing fresco called Pan American Unity by the Mexican muralist Rivera, which became a subject of study for her students. Later, she expanded the mural survey to include, among others, two additional SF frescoes by Rivera at the SF Art Institute and The City Club; thus she had an opportunity to analyze Rivera's sometimes hidden political and religious symbols. These will be the subjects of today's slide lecture.
