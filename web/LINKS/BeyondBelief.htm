Bringing up children without religious influence seems daunting.  Despite the difficulties, a large and growing number of parents are choosing to raise their kids without religion.  According to the 2001 American Religious Identification Survey, 14% of respondents identified themselves as non-religious.  Dale McGowan, editor of PARENTING BEYOND BELIEF: On Raising Ethical, Caring Kids Without Religion (AMACOM; April 26, 2007; $17.95, paperback), estimates that seven million non-religious parents are raising children in the U.S. today and doing so with limited resources.

In PARENTING BEYOND BELIEF, more than 30 contributors celebrate the freedom that comes with raising kids without formal indoctrination and advise parents on effective ways to raise caring, independent, and open minded children.  As McGowan observes, "This book is intended to support and encourage those who, having already decided to raise their children without religion, are in search of that support and encouragement."

From the humorous "Little Bessie Would Assist Providence" to the poignant, "Dealing With Death in the Secular Family," these contributions offer guidance on the common issues parents find themselves facing everyday.  Here parents will also find wise guidance on such topics as being and doing good, meaning and purpose, questioning, the wonder of science, and seeking community.  Sensitive and timely, PARENTING BEYOND BELIEF is the first book to show parents that they can raise independent, ethical children with confidence and without fear.
