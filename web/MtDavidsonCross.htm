San Diego's Cross Transfer Declared Unconstitutional --
San Francisco's Mt. Davidson Cross Suit Continues!

After waiting nine months, American Atheists' suit against the transfer of San Francisco's Mt. Davidson cross and surrounding park land is finally moving forward.

Last September, the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals ruled against American Atheists' claim that the transfer as structured was unconstitutional.  That decision was based on a precedent set by a lawsuit against a similar transfer of a public cross called the Mt. Soledad Cross in San Diego.

In October, both American Atheists and the San Diego plaintiff requested an en banc review of their respective suits.  An en banc review is a review of the decision by an 11-judge panel.  Since the Mt. Davidson decision was dependent upon the outcome of the San Diego case (Paulson v. City of San Diego), the court then put the Mt. Davidson decision on hold until the San Diego case had a final determination.

The court held the en banc hearing for Paulson in Phoenix on March 21.  On June 26, they reversed the original 3-judge panel decision and sent the case back to the district court for a remedy.

The court determined that the City, by requiring that the land be used for a war memorial, was providing a subsidy for parties wanting to use the cross as their memorial.  In other words, if two parties - one wanting to keep the cross and the other wanting to remove it - both have the same amount of money to spend, the party wishing to keep the cross standing would have a financial advantage in the bidding process, because they could bid all of their available resources, whereas the party wishing to remove the cross would have to reserve funds in order to both remove the cross and then erect a suitable war memorial.

In conclusion, the Ninth Circuit en banc panel stated:

"The second sale of the Mt. Soledad land on which the cross stands was structured to provide a direct, immediate, and substantial financial advantage to bidders who had the sectarian purpose of preserving the cross.  For that reason, the sale violated article XVI, section 5 of the California Constitution."

Now that the precedent has been reversed, the court is once again looking at the Mt. Davidson transfer.  All parties involved in the Mt. Davidson action have 3 weeks in which to file a brief explaining how the Paulson decision affects our case.

Plaintiffs Dave Kong (CA director for American Atheists) and John Messina are very pleased that the suit is going forward, and appreciate the support that so many have shown.  They want to thank everyone who contributed to the en banc filing, who made this next step possible.
